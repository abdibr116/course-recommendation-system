For this assignment, you are required to prepare and submit a Final Project Report based on the project idea you developed during the first phase and the proposal you submitted earlier.
Your final report should clearly outline the following parts:
1. Project Title
A short, clear, and descriptive title that reflects the focus of your project.
2. Abstract
A concise summary (150 words max) of your entire project. It should briefly summarize your work. The abstract is written last but placed at the beginning of the report.
3. Introduction
A brief overview of your project idea which explains the context: why this project is relevant or interesting? Make sure to keep it short but engaging to set the stage for the rest of the proposal.
4. Problem Statement
It defines the issue or gap your project aims to address and explain why this problem is important and worth solving. it should also provide background information or examples to make the problem clear.
5. Objectives
List the specific goals or tasks achieved through your project. Think of objectives as the "roadmap" for your project.
6.  Literature Review / Background 
Summarize related work, existing solutions, or relevant studies that connect to your project.
Highlight how your project builds on or differs from these efforts.
7. Methodology/Implementation 
Provide a detailed explanation of how you conducted your project.
Describe the tools, techniques, frameworks, or methods you used.
Explain the steps you followed in developing, testing, or implementing your solution.
8. Evaluation and Results
Explain how your project’s performance or success was measured.
Present the evaluation criteria, experimental design, testing methods, and metrics you used (e.g., accuracy, efficiency, response time, user feedback, etc.).
Present key results obtained during testing or experimentation using tables, figures, or charts.
9. Results Analysis and Discussion
Analyze and interpret the results presented in the previous section.
Discuss what the outcomes mean in relation to your objectives.
Highlight key insights, identify strengths and weaknesses, and explain any unexpected findings or limitations.
Connect your discussion to related work or theoretical frameworks if relevant.
10. Conclusion and Future Work
Summarize your overall findings and main achievements. Reflect on whether your objectives were met and the contribution of your project.
Suggest possible improvements, extensions, or future research directions.
11. References
Provide a properly formatted list of all references cited in your report  (IEEE, APA, or any other formats).
Submission Guidelines
Format: Word or PDF
Length: 5–10 pages
